## Safety Guidelines

- **Do Not Operate** machine without reading this manual thoroughly.  
- Keep hands away from the kettle during popping—temperatures exceed **200°C (392°F)**.  
- Use only grounded outlets rated **120V 15A**.  
- Disconnect power before servicing or cleaning.  
- Never immerse the machine’s base or electrical components in water.  

⚠️ **Warning:** Failure to follow safety instructions may result in burns, electrical shock, or equipment damage.

## Installation

1. **Unpack Carefully**  
   Remove all packaging materials and inspect for transport damage.  
2. **Place on Level Surface**  
   Ensure the unit sits on a flat, heat-resistant surface.  
3. **Connect Power**  
   Plug into a grounded 120V outlet.  
4. **Attach Oil Line**  
   Connect the clear silicone oil line to the kettle inlet. Ensure no kinks.  
5. **Prime Oil System**  
   Hold the *Prime* button for 3 seconds to charge the feed line.  

✅ The machine is now ready for operation.

---

## Operation

1. **Power On**  
   Switch the main power toggle to *ON*. The SmartHeat indicator should glow amber.  
2. **Preheat Kettle**  
   Wait until the indicator turns green (~3 minutes).  
3. **Load Ingredients**  
   - Add ½ cup kernels and 1 oz. of preferred oil.  
   - Optional: Sprinkle salt or seasoning mix.  
4. **Press START POP**  
   The popping cycle runs automatically (~4 minutes).  
5. **Serve**  
   Once complete, tilt the kettle to release popcorn into the drawer.  

💡 *Tip:* For continuous production, pre-measure kernel/oil packets for each batch.

## Cleaning & Maintenance

- **Daily:**  
  - Unplug machine.  
  - Wipe kettle exterior with a damp cloth.  
  - Empty drawer and clean with mild detergent.  
- **Weekly:**  
  - Remove and wash the filter tray.  
  - Check oil line integrity.  
- **Monthly (Field Service):**  
  - Inspect all connectors for wear or corrosion.  
  - Verify temperature calibration using a kettle probe.

## Troubleshooting

| Symptom | Possible Cause | Solution |
|----------|----------------|----------|
| Machine won’t power on | Power cord unplugged, fuse blown | Check outlet, replace 10A fuse |
| Kettle not heating | Faulty thermostat, loose connector | Inspect wiring; replace thermostat if open |
| Popcorn burns easily | Thermostat miscalibrated | Run calibration cycle (see Field Service) |
| Oil pump not operating | Air in line or clogged filter | Prime oil system; replace filter |
| Popcorn doesn’t pop fully | Old kernels, temperature too low | Use fresh kernels, verify 200°C kettle temp |
| Excess smoke | Oil buildup in kettle | Perform deep clean of kettle interior |

## Field Service Procedures

### Required Tools
- Multimeter  
- 10mm wrench  
- Silicone sealant  
- Temperature probe (0–300°C range)  

### 1. Electrical Inspection
- Disconnect power.  
- Remove rear access panel.  
- Check for loose or burnt connectors.  
- Measure resistance of kettle element: nominal **18.5Ω ±5%**.

### 2. Temperature Calibration
- Power the machine and run in *Service Mode* (hold START 10s).  
- Insert temperature probe in kettle.  
- Adjust control knob until probe reads 200°C ±2°C.

### 3. Oil Pump Replacement
1. Disconnect oil line and pump harness.  
2. Remove 2 mounting screws.  
3. Install replacement pump (Part# SP5K-OILPMP).  
4. Prime system for 5 seconds before operation.
